## Quoting Workflow

This is how a quote moves from enquiry to delivery:

1. **Request comes in** — usually via email from the customer, with scope notes attached or in the body.
2. **Supporting documents** — sometimes includes a BOQ or scope spreadsheet (e.g. meter schedules in XLSX format).
3. **Scope review** — I review the scope and reference prior similar quotes and estimators for pricing guidance.
4. **Build estimator** — I build the job estimator in Excel, using prior workbook patterns as a base.
5. **Produce quote document** — I generate a Word or PDF quote in the SKS format.
6. **Assign quote number** — Quote numbers are manually assigned from the SKS system. **This cannot be automated. It is a hard constraint.**
7. **Send to customer** — Quote is reviewed, numbered, and sent.

**Previous system:** Simpro (no longer in use). We are back to Excel + Word.

**File storage:** OneDrive (corporate Microsoft 365 environment).

---

## Current Quoting Pain Points

- No consistent quoting process — each quote is rebuilt largely from scratch.
- Manual Excel/Word workflow is slow and error-prone.
- Institutional knowledge lives in old estimators and the heads of a few people — it gets lost between quotes.
- No memory of prior pricing decisions, labour rates, or material costs across jobs.
- Hard to maintain consistency in quote formatting and presentation.

---

## What AI Should Help With

- **Read incoming scope emails and attached XLSX files** (e.g. meter schedules, BOQs) and extract key scope details.
- **Reference prior estimators** to suggest labour hours, materials, and pricing based on similar past jobs.
- **Draft the estimator structure** in Excel, pre-populated based on the scope, ready for my review and adjustment.
- **Produce a quote document** in SKS Word/PDF format, ready for me to review.
- **Preserve institutional knowledge** — surface relevant decisions, rates, and notes from past quotes so nothing gets lost.
- AI should always present work as a **draft for my review**. I assign the quote number manually from the SKS system before anything goes to a customer.

---

## Hard Constraints

- **Quote numbers must be assigned manually from the SKS system.** AI must never generate, suggest, or auto-assign quote numbers.
- All final documents go through me before reaching the customer.
