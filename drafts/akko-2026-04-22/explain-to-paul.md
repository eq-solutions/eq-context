# What we built for Akko — explained like you're 8

## The big idea

Imagine your dad does a really big job at someone's house. He puts in special light switches and clever buttons that talk to each other through wires in the walls. That's called a **KNX system**.

When the job is finished, he has to give the family a big book that proves everything works — every switch, every light, every plug. Right now, making that book takes ages. He has to copy lots of bits from his computer, take photos with his phone, write notes in a notebook, then squish it all together at his desk later.

We made him a **magic worksheet** he can carry on his iPad. As he walks around the house, he taps the worksheet. By the time he leaves, the book is already nearly done.

## What the magic worksheet can do

**It remembers everything by itself.** He never has to press "save". A little dot in the corner tells him "Saved just now" so he knows it's safe.

**It has tick-boxes for every test.** When he tests a light, he taps Pass. The worksheet keeps a tally — "12 of 30 done" — so he can see how much is left.

**It can read the special KNX file** that comes from the design computer. Drop it in and the worksheet fills in hundreds of rows for him in one go.

**He can take a photo and draw on it.** A red circle around the loose cable. A blue arrow pointing at the wonky switch. The drawing stays on the photo forever.

**The customer can sign on the screen** with their finger when they're happy. The signature goes straight into the book.

**If he taps Delete by accident, he can un-delete.** A little "Undo" button waits 8 seconds before the thing really goes away.

**At the end he taps one button** and out pops a beautiful PDF book with the company logo on every page, ready to email to the family.

## Why this matters for Paul

The job that used to take **a whole afternoon at the desk after site** now finishes **before the van leaves the driveway**. That means more jobs done, fewer late nights, and customers who get their handover pack the same day they get their lights.

It runs in any web browser. No internet needed on site. Nothing to install. Works on every iPad Akko already owns. Akko's logo, Akko's colours, Akko's process — it's their tool, not someone else's software with their name stuck on the side.

## The one-line pitch

**It turns "the boring paperwork bit at the end of every job" into something that's already done before you pack up the ladder.**
